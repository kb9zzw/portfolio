    Introduction

    Being able to extract features from remote sensing data is a powerful way to analyze imagery and monitor vast areas for environmental change. In this example, supervised image classification is employed to identify various categories of land or wetland types. This particular technique uses small samples which train the software to identify areas that are similar in spectral characterstics. The technique is rarely perfect, but major features such as water or forest are distinguishable from other areas such as cultivated land or bare areas.

    Classification below was performed using ArcGIS Desktop.

    Classification Results

    This show the results of running supervised classification using the sample polygons from the previous image. Classification generally improves as more sample polygons are added. For example, slide the slider to the left to see that cultivated areas are more readily identified. However, even with more samples, the classification is not perfect.

    Problem Areas

    Satellite images often have shadows and other image artifacts that interfere with classification techniques. This image has a shadow area along the tree line separating the forested area from cultivated areas. This section is difficult for the software to classify and may require human digitization.
