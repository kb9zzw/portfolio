    Introduction

    I've been working for the past three years on achieving the Master of Geospatial Information Science (MGIST) degree from the Center for Geospatial Analytics at North Carolina State University. During my student career, I have been challenged to stretch my imagination on what GIS is and how it can be used. Looking back, I am amazed at the variety of scenarios where GIS technologies can be applied.

    What is GIScience?

    One of the concepts stressed early in the MGIST program was the concept of GIScience. It is easy to identify GIS with the tools and technologies used for cartography, mapping and analysis. But GIScience goes far beyond the tools. It stresses the application of scientific methods to geospatial problems. As I have learned, it can be quite helpful to think about scientific problems in a geospatial context. Most scientific problems have a "where", a location in time or space that can be investigated. GIScience brings a mindset of location to these problems, using principles of spatial analyisis, statistics and fundamentals of geography to bear on solving the problem. This can be found in many of the tools used in GIS today.

    Applications of GIScience

    The applications of GIS and GIScience are nearly endless, as I've learned. While my background is in environmental analysis, specifically meteorology and climatology, my peers were often using GIS for wildy different applications. Business applications, such as real estate development, were common. Forest management, oceanography, political science were also common. If a problem has a location, GIScience can almost certainly be used to address that problem.

    GIS @ NC State University

    Throughout the last 3 years, I've had tremendous opportunity to explore the GIScience space. At first, it started with becoming familiar with the tools and language of GIS. By the end, it has grown into a mature understanding of geopatial theory and the ability to apply this theory to model real-world environments. This can be seen throughout this site in the application of GIS to climate change, one of my focus areas.

    I invite you to browse this site, which compiles some of the work that I have performed during my career in the MGIST program at NC State University. My hope is that this showcases a broad variety of skills and technologies I've acquired during my studies. I also invite you to visit the Center for Geospatial Analytics, where work continues on cutting-edge GIScience and related technologies.
