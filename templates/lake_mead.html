    Introduction

    Large portions of the western United States have been experiencing periods of persistent drought during the past decade. Lower than normal precipitation has had wide-reaching impacts on water resources and agriculture. This project looked at drought impacts on Lake Mead from 2013 to 2014. Drought conditions at Lake Mead have caused dramatic decrease in water levels. The decreasing water levels were especially noticeable in 2013-2014.

    This study was a "before and after" comparison of Lake Mead lake levels from LandSat imagery of acquired from March 24, 2013 and March 15, 2014. Each image was analyzed using unsupervised classification to extract the water features from the surrounding land features. The surface area of the water features was calculated for each time period. This showed a significant contraction of water area during the year.

    Classification Process

    Analysis performed using ERDAS Imagine 2013

    Lake Mead water feature extracted using:

    • Unsupervised classification to distinguish water from land
    • Recoding, masking to identify and extract Lake Mead
    • Calculate surface area of water pixels identified as Lake Mead

    Why unsupervised classification?

    Unsupervised image classification was performed due to simplicity of operation. Also, this method is effective when working with high-contrast features, such as water versus land in Band 4 (near infrared)

    Results

    Analysis showed a decrease of lake surface area by almost 5% in one year of a decade's long drought. Images below show before (dark blue) and after (aqua) lake extent.

    Las Vegas Bay

    The relatively shallow Las Vegas Bay showed significant contraction, including formation of land bridges.

    Lake Mead Marina

    Shore line near the Lake Mead marina contracted by up to 100m in some locations.

    Overton, Gulf "Islands"

    What were previously islands are now connected to the mainland. 2014 drought strenthened this land bridge.

    Pool Islands / Bonell Bay

    Shoreline contraction varies depending on shore steepness.
