    Introduction

    Remote sensing provides a powerful way to investigate patterns and trends in the environment without direct measurement. Spectral properties of sensors can be used to extract information about a particular area. There are many types of remote sensing platforms. Satellites provide a view from space and offer the benefit of being able to monitor areas nearly continuously or with frequent revisits. While sensor packages on satellites are getting increasingly sophisticated, there are limitations due to altitude and general inaccessibility for repair and modification. Other remote sensing platforms, such as aerial imagery and LIDAR can be used to supplement the vast amount of information from satellites.

    Radiometric Enhancement

    Radiometric enhancement can be used to highlight features and extract them from the surrounding noise. In this case, the goal was to highlight the confluence of two rivers, the Missouri and Mississipi. The image started off as an unstretched RGB Landsat 4 image. Spectral profile points were added identify spectral signatures of both rivers. The result is an image where the water features are enhanced from the surrounding landscape.
